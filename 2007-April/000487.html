I took a look at the HAL structure for some time this morning, and there are
a lot of open design issues. Using the parport infrastructure requires to store
a lot of things not foreseen in the present HAL, at least as I understand
it. An example is the result of parport_register_device() and the peak
driver has a whole structure (DONGLE_PORT) for that. Implementing
this, and hopefully in a sensible way, will take me some time and effort at
the edge of my programming knowledge. So I am glad for Oliver's offer and
willing to test and help.

Otherwise, as I had transmission errors with the original can200 dongle and
the original CANBUS4LINUX driver, I think it is a good idea to test the
hardware in a well tested driver environment with minimal code change
nescessary. The peak driver provides this environment.

B.t.w, if the extended hal layer could keep in mind other possible transport
protocolls ... With USB as protocoll I could finally get my hardware after
figure 32 in the ftdichip.com DS_FT2232D datasheet to work ;-)
