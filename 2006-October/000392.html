Oliver Hartkopp <socketcan at hartkopp.net> writes:

> this leads to the problem, that i need to add <sys/socket.h> in 
> trunk/can-utils/lib.c again.
> 
> The sa_family_t from <linux/socket.h> is not accessible from user-apps 
> due to some #ifdef statements.
> 
> I think, the idea was to only include <linux/can.h> in user-apps. So 
> what would be the correct way to fix this problem?
> Writing some
> 
> #ifdef __KERNEL__
> #include <linux/socket.h>
> #else
> #include <sys/socket.h>
> #endif
> 
> into can.h ?

sa_family_t is defined for user space in <sys/socket.h>.  The
definition in <linux/socket.h> is for kernel space only.  Therefore, I
suggest to include <sys/socket.h> in our CAN user space programs, in
addition to and before <linux/can.h>.
