Hi Uwe,

the question is not about *who* has to handle this BUS_OFF event. This 
has been already clarified in several discussions:

- You can handle the BUS_OFF event by the application
- You can handle the BUS_OFF event by the driver (automatic restart 
after x ms)

You can decide this on your own just as needed in your specific 
use-case. Looks like the handling on application level is your favorite ;-)

Btw. Wolfgang made an excellent summary here:
https://lists.berlios.de/pipermail/socketcan-core/2008-January/001039.html

But coming back to Wolfgangs original question:

I had some problems with wrong XTAL specifications where i ran into 
BUS_OFF many times, that caused the former brutal error handling in the 
old SJA1000 driver. At first glance your patch adds proper state 
transitions, i did not pay attention to in my implementation :-]
