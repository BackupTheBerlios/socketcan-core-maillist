Btw. i had a discussion at lunch with my colleagues that know well about 
TTCAN. The result was, that if you operate a CAN bus in a time triggered 
way, you should (at least) not have any arbitration problems or things 
like that. And it looks also very curious to operate a CAN bus 
Time-Triggered an in parallel to allow 'normal' CAN-frames that would be 
able to resend on errors. This breaks with the TTCAN concept in whole. 
After this discussion i'm *really* unsure if there's a use-case for a 
socket-option and a per-socket setting. It's not that i'm not willing to 
create a new socket-option BUT it has to be a use-case behind it. Just 
to implement things because we can implement it easily is a bad approach.

@Chris: Please check out, why you think a per-socket option is a real 
recommendation and why it cannot be solved on driver level (separately 
for each bus). In other words: Maybe your client just requires to send 
single-shot frames and not to send single-shot frames AND normal 
CAN-frames at the same time on the identical bus.
