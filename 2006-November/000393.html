Urs Thuermann wrote:
> Wolfgang Grandegger <wg at grandegger.com> writes:
> 
>>>> If you do not read, you do not need to set any filters. So your preferred
>>>> behaviour is just implemented.
>>> And not reading means not binding, correct? So an unbound socket is
>>> write-only and doesn't consume resources wrt packet reception.
>> You have to bind the socket to an interface. Otherwise ifindex is 0
>> which could mean sending to all interfaces. But it's not implemented
>> in my RTCAN. Nevertheless, is this something we would like to support?
> 
> I am surprised we have this discussion.  We are all familiar with the
> socket API, aren't we?  There is not such thing as a write-only
> socket.  You open a socket and can read and write from/to it.  If you
> don't want to read, well than don't read.  No problem at all.
> Received packets are queued until the queue is full and then
> discarded.  Nothing surprising or unusual.  Just like e.g. UDP.
> 
> Moreover, you can sendto() a PF_CAN/RAW socket without having to bind
> to an interface.  That means you can open a socket, won't receive
> anything but you can nevertheless write to it.  If you want to use
> send() instead of sendto() you have to bind() to an interface and just
> let the kernel drop the packets for you.  So what is the problem?

Is the setting of an empty filter list with setsockopt legal (size=0)? 
This could be another way to avoid the default filter entry accepting 
all messages.
