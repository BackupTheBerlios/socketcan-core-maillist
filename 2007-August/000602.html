Hans-Jürgen Koch wrote:
> BTW, today I ordered a PCAN-USB gadget from www.peak-system.com for testing
> purposes. They provide Linux drivers for char devices and for SocketCAN as
> well.
>   

I maintained a SocketCAN support patch for the PEAK driver for a long
time just for our own purposes. It was a great step from the PEAK guys
to make their USB-driver GPL and to include the netdev support into
their mainline driver. Thanks again.

> Any chance to merge the latter into the socketcan tree?
>   

Hm - the current PEAK driver is a big block supporting chardev, netdev
and RT interfaces for a bunch of different CAN hardware devices. So i
don't assume the PEAK driver can go into the kernel with it's current
implementation. I think it should be split up in tiny drivers for a
specific hardware then (only supporting the netdev interface).
Btw. i moved the current PEAK-USB-part into my test kernel tree as a
proof-of-concept and it worked fine. But it was a rough work in some
cases that should not be posted at all ;-)
