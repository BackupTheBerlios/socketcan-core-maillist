I must admit, that, reading the discussion on linux-netdev, I
understand why you feel that way and I might also, if I were to read
these mails without knowing the people.  OTOH, I know Oliver and I
don't think it was his intention to disturb you or to push you to
integrate our code into the kernel without necessary changes being
done.  Some misunderstandings might be the result of infelicitous
formulations.  Unfortunately, I wasn't able to immediately answer on
the list to try to help the situation.

Also, we know that you do all the work voluntarily and we really value
that effort very well.  Really.  Actually, although we started that
project at our daytime job and I wouldn't have ever got in contact
with CAN without my job, we do much on it in our spare time, now.  I
don't like CAN but I know many people use it in embedded systems
running linux.  I therefore would like to have a good CAN
implementation in the linux kernel.  So our main goal is not to have
our code integrated into the kernel by all means as it might seem from
some mails on the list, but to provide the best implementation
possible and that's why I liked the feedback we got from the list.
Then, of course, we also try to get our code into the main kernel so
that more people can use it more easily.

I hope I could clarify some things, ease this trouble and we can still
work together.
