On Sun, Jun 08, 2008 at 11:42:10PM +0200, Hans J. Koch wrote:
> I agree, but only in a hardware-independent way. Calculating register
> values in userspace is a no-go. From a userspace app we want to open a
> socket and configure the bitrate without having to care about what
> chip is behind it. If that were not possible, we would lose much of
> the advantages of having PF_CAN in the networking core.

Right, I thought that was the reasoning behind the way I described
earlier in this thread. But have to re-read the other thread first...

Configuring the bitrate is something which is usually done at "ifup"
time, so having a "canconfig" tool to do things in userspace is not
generally a no-no. But I agree with Hans that directly feeding some
device independend parameter set into the kernel is much better.

> What do I do in at91_can? I don't have btr0 or btr1. Do you really want
> to export chip specific registers through sysfs? Then we will see loads
> of userspace programs that only work with certain chips (similar to good
> old DOS times when printer drivers were compiled into the application).

We had lots of discussions with CAN people before Juergen implemented
that interface, and it turned out that it represents a way that

- can be implemented easily in a driver
- makes it possible to get standard conformant bitrate deviations
- offers an easy way to set just "the" bitrate (using a table
  internally)
- seems to fulfill anything needed by non-sja-type controllers
- uses a model which comes directly from the CAN standard and thus is
  well understood by CAN people
