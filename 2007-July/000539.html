Oliver Hartkopp <socketcan at hartkopp.net> writes:

> Of course you may use container_of() if you feel better with it - and it
> is definitely correct to do so.

No, not even that.  If you define

        struct sja1000_priv {
                foo_t foo;
                struct can_priv can;
                bar_t bar;
        };

you can use container_of() to get from the pointer struct can_priv *cp
to the surronding sja1000_priv struct, like in

        sp = container_of(cp, struct sja1000_priv, can);

but you still have no way to get from the *dev to the pointer to the
struct can_priv.  The only way would be to have struct can_priv not be
part of struct sja1000_priv and instead append sja1000_priv to
can_priv, with or without a pointer in can_priv to the following
sja1000_priv.  I described why I don't like that in my other mail.
