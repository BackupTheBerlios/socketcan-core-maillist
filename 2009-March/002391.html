Oliver Hartkopp <socketcan at hartkopp.net> wrote:

> Do you have any better ideas?

Not really, I just feel the need to make the Project more visible as
the single most useful up-to-date CAN-Bus support available for Linux.

Mainline inclusion will certainly help with this. But something
comparable to the "Real-Time Linux Wiki" would be nice to have.

I think about a place for putting small HOWTO and FAQ stuff. Simple
RX and TX code, etc.

I could for example contibute an example on how to bring up a can
device the debian way (/etc/network/interfaces):

auto can0
iface can0 inet manual
	# bitrate 1MBps
	pre-up echo 1000000 >/sys/class/net/$IFACE/can_bittiming/bitrate
	# increase TX queue length because of the pulsed nature of
	# the traffic we generate
	pre-up echo 1000 >/sys/class/net/$IFACE/tx_queue_len
	up /sbin/ifconfig $IFACE up
	down /sbin/ifconfig $IFACE down

If I would know where to put it :)
