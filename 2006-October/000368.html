> Do you mean the hardware loopback of the controller? Good idea,
> indeed. I just checked with MSCAN. In loopback mode nothing goes out
> to the CAN bus (because it's an internal loopback). Unfortunately,
> there is no "self-reception request" like on the SJA1000 :-(.
> Nevertheless, I fully support this proposal as it saves code.

No, the discussion was about some kind of lo (software) device, just for
CAN, and the question where the looping functionality has to be placed.
No real hardware-internal packet looping involved.

The main question is: are there users out there who might run into the
situation that, if you have system A and B and processes I und II, the
processes have problems with packet order if

a) I runs on A and II runs on A
b) I runs on A and II runs on B

and some other code send s packets on A, because in case of arbitration
delayed packets due to higher priority incoming packets the packet order
on the local loopback interface would be different than on the other
system.

We couldn't imagine use cases which could be problematic, but that
doesn't mean there are none.
