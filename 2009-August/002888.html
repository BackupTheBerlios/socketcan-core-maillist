I think we have utilities for various purposes:

- official utilities for sending and receiving messages. Sooner than
  later we also need programs using netlink for configuration and
  restart, similar too canconfig.
- regression testing
- benchmarking
- examples

I actually like Olivers utilities, as they have a lot of functionality,
even fancy ones, like coloring. [BTW: what I don't like is the unusual
way of defining the utility options]. But they are not good examples to
look at because they are too complex and need an extra library. The
Pengutronix utilities are better for that purpose and for that reason I
would like to keep canecho and cansequence.  So, as I see it, it's
mainly a matter of putting them into the right directory and add some
documentation:

 can-utils/          Offical CAN utilities, like candump, cansend, etc.
 can-utils/examples  cansequence, canecho, canrtt, Valdislav's
                     canecho_dut, canecho_gen, etc.

 can-utils/test      As is.
