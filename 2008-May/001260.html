I disagree on this point....
See the wireless people (one example).
During the last weeks they have sent maybe 5 pull requests to
David Miller. And all was pulled within hours or in a few cases days.

You may in no way compare the submission of a new net subsystem (CAN)
with submission of fixes/updates to an existing driver. Submission
of new drivers from the CAN driver maintainer will also go much smoother
when the CAN maintainer has shown decent quality in past submissions.

I bet that you will find things much easier to deal with the day
you pull the trigger and drop your private SVN repository for
the CAN development.

There are good reasons to do so now - but when this driver stuff
is done I see no reason to keep the kernel stuff in SVN.

People on the list will likely raise the backward compatiblity
argument here - but the counter argument is to upgrade the kernel.
And if these people really need to be compatible with an older kernel
then they have also decided they can spend money on it.
